In a major step toward strengthening renewable energy capacity, the Government of India announced a new initiative aimed at accelerating the installation of rooftop solar systems in urban areas. The Ministry of New and Renewable Energy stated that the program will provide financial incentives and technical support for households and small businesses that adopt solar power systems.

According to officials, the initiative is designed to help India move closer to its national goal of achieving 500 gigawatts of renewable energy capacity by 2030. Under the new plan, homeowners installing rooftop solar panels will receive subsidies covering up to 40 percent of installation costs. In addition, banks and financial institutions have been encouraged to provide low-interest loans to support the adoption of solar technology.

Energy experts have welcomed the initiative, noting that rooftop solar can significantly reduce dependence on fossil fuels and lower electricity bills for consumers. “Decentralized renewable energy solutions like rooftop solar are critical for improving energy security and reducing carbon emissions,” said an energy policy analyst at a leading research institute.

The government also plans to launch awareness campaigns in major cities to educate citizens about the environmental and financial benefits of renewable energy. Officials believe that increased participation from households and businesses will play a crucial role in meeting the country’s long-term sustainability goals.

Industry stakeholders have also expressed optimism that the policy will create new opportunities in the renewable energy sector, including jobs in manufacturing, installation, and maintenance of solar equipment.
