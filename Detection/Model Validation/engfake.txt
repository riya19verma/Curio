A controversial report circulating on several online platforms claims that a secret international agreement will soon require all citizens to install government-controlled surveillance devices inside their homes. According to the article, the devices would supposedly monitor electricity usage, internet activity, and even private conversations in order to enforce new global environmental regulations.

The report alleges that the agreement was negotiated during a closed-door meeting between world leaders and major technology corporations. It claims that governments plan to deploy smart monitoring devices disguised as “energy efficiency meters” and that the devices will transmit data directly to centralized government databases.

The article further suggests that individuals who refuse to install the devices could face penalties such as higher electricity tariffs, restricted internet access, or financial fines. It also claims that several countries have already begun testing the technology without informing the public.

However, experts have raised serious doubts about the authenticity of these claims. No official announcements or credible reports from recognized institutions have confirmed the existence of such an agreement. Technology analysts note that many of the technical details described in the article are unrealistic and inconsistent with current infrastructure.

Despite the lack of evidence, the story has spread rapidly across social media platforms, highlighting the challenges governments and organizations face in combating misinformation online.
